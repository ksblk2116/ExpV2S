ExpV2S



Speech conveys a wealth of information beyond spoken words through vocal attributes such as emotion, pitch, age, and gender, which are often overlooked by current lip-to-speech (L2S) methods. To bridge this gap, we introduce a novel task called expressive video-to-speech synthesis (EV2S), which aims to generate expressive speech from silent talking videos. Unlike traditional L2S, which focuses solely on producing intelligible speech, EV2S requires that the generated output not only synchronizes with lip motions but also aligns with facial movements. A key challenge in this task is to exploit the relationship between facial movements and vocal attributes while preserving the integrity of the content. To overcome this, we categorize facial movements into two components: i) emotion-related styles—including emotion and pitch, which can vary over time, and ii) speaker characteristcs—such as gender, accent, and age, which remain constant. We propose a disentangling strategy that implicitly separates facial information into speaker and emotion embeddings. Building upon this framework, we develop a latent diffusion-based method for Expressive Video-to-Speech synthesis (ExpV2S), which conditions on these embeddings alongside content embeddings extracted from lip motion. Guided by these conditions, ExpV2S enables to control the spoken words, emotion styles and speaker characteristics in the speech. We introduce two benchmarks: MEAD and CREMA, derived from emotional talking head synthesis datasets. Extensive experiments demonstrate that ExpV2S is capable of generating more expressive speech than existing lip-to-speech and text-to-speech methods, as evidenced by both quantitative metrics and human evaluations
